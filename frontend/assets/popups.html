Welcome to the Route Visualizer project!

Drag and drop

"Ignoring the signs is a good way to end up at the wrong destination."

The green flag is our landing site, and destination is at the red flag! (Cool stuff, right?)

Drag and drop any flag to test out several different locations for our source and destination.

Hills and valleys

"Obstacles. Most can be overcome by the appropriate use of explosives."

Left click and move your mouse over an area to add/remove obstacles. Our visualizer won't be able to move over these spots :(

Algorithms

"A word used by programmers when they don't want to explain what they did."

Open the side panel and explore 11 different algorithms.

Note that some nice algorithms will always find the shortest path (like A*), and some not-so-nice algorithms might take a longer walk (like beam search).

Modes

"To be or not to be? I want to know where to be."

Multiple sources: Test out several possible landing sites by placing multiple sources.
Multiple destinations: Find out the nearest red flag by placing multiple destinations.
Checkpoints: Need a detour? Place checkpoints and our visualizer will visit them in order.

Right click to add or remove these extra flags from any mode.

Configurations

"Variety is the spice of life. And algorithms as well."

Tinker around with different settings in our simulator. Note that some of these might be disabled based on the algorithm and other configurations.

Allow diagonals: Specify whether a diagonal movement is allowed.
Cut corners: Whether the visualizer is allowed to cut the corner of an obstacle.
Bidirectional: Specify whether the destination is a moving agent or not.

Define sunlight

"A day without sunshine is like, you know, night."

Our visualizer runs on solar energy and would prefer walking on sunshine.

Click on define sunlight and move the slider to adjust intensity. Left click and move over any area to assign the chosen intensity.

Note that unweighted graph algorithms like jump point search will be unaffected by this setting.

Wormholes

"If I could teleport, I'd probably still be late."

One of our engineers is a big sci-fi fan, and hopes to find teleportation wormholes on Mars someday.

Sometimes our destination might be unreachable, but don't you worry! Play around with our wormhole feature to teleport from one place to another. Note that this might affect the usual properties associated with any chosen algorithm.

Random mazes

"So much of life is determined by pure randomness."

Sometimes the journey can be unpredictable and we need to prepare for that.

Select one of our maze generation algorithms and click on Random Maze to generate a random terrain.

Let's go!

Click on Find Route to get going, and Reset Grid to restore the default settings.

Go forth and conquer, Martian. Make us proud.
